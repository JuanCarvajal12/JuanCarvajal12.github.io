## BARCo: Bringing Astronomy to Rural Communities of Colombia

BARCo provides <strong>ready-to-use classroom activities</strong> designed by astronomers, packaged as an offline-friendly <strong>kit</strong> with teacher-facing guides. The emphasis is hands-on and realistic: activities that can be run in real classrooms with limited resources.

The materials are openly available so they can be replicated elsewhere:
<p class="link-row">
  <a href="https://www.astroreca.org/en/barco-astronomia-rural-colombia/kit-barco" target="_blank" rel="noopener">BARCo kit</a>
  <a href="https://zenodo.org/records/13738441" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Zenodo record</a>
</p>

<div class="outreach-grid">

  <div class="outreach-card">
    <h3>What it does</h3>
    <p>
      BARCo brings astronomy activities to rural schools using offline-friendly materials, teacher guides, and practical classroom activities.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="outreach-card">
    <h3>Why it matters</h3>
    <p>
      Many rural communities in Colombia face long-standing infrastructure gaps, limited internet access, and histories marked by violence and insecurity. These regions often feel forgotten by the central government.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="outreach-card">
    <h3>My role</h3>
    <p>
      I contributed as a volunteer by helping design activities, writing and editing booklets, contacting schools, and supporting teachers during implementation.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

The project was conceptualized in 2023 and implemented in 2024 with support from the <strong>IAU Office of Astronomy for Development</strong>. Most schools continued receiving support from RECA Educación in 2025.

BARCo was built to reach communities that are often missed by standard outreach formats. The point was simple: give teachers something usable, offline, and classroom-ready, so students can access a science experience that is usually concentrated in large cities.
