# NGC 1427A: a dwarf caught in transformation

This page summarizes my first-author paper on NGC 1427A, a disturbed star-forming dwarf galaxy in the Fornax Cluster.

<p class="link-row">
  <a href="https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2026arXiv260500988C/abstract" target="_blank" rel="noopener">ADS</a>
  <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00988" target="_blank" rel="noopener">arXiv</a>

## The problem

Cluster dwarf galaxies are usually gas-poor today, but the transition from gas-rich to gas-poor is short and difficult to observe directly. NGC 1427A offers a useful snapshot of this transition because it still has gas and active star formation, but already shows strong signs of environmental disturbance.

---

## What we did

We combined a deep, spatially contiguous VLT/MUSE mosaic with ancillary data from the FUV to radio. This let us map stars, ionised gas, H&nbsp;I, dust, recent star formation, and stellar populations in the same system.

---

## Main result

The stars and gas are strongly decoupled. The gas phases are tilted relative to the stellar kinematics and are partly blueshifted. Dust and attenuation constraints indicate that gas and dust are being pushed partly along the line of sight.

We interpret this as a mixed scenario: a mild dwarf-dwarf fly-by likely disturbed or pre-conditioned the gas, while ram pressure from the Fornax intracluster medium is now stripping the ISM.
